The Art Lover

With Richmond being home to Virginia Commonwealth University, or VCU, and the Virginia Museum of Fine Arts, art has always been an attraction for visiting. There is too much to see in one day but we will get you started with some of the main attractions.

9:00-10:30 a.m. Breakfast in Carytown at a restaurant of your choice.
10:30-1:00 p.m. Visit the VMFA, which stands for the Virginia Museum of Fine Arts, to view a permanent collection of over 33,000 pieces of ancient to modern art. Also, the VMFA hosts visiting exhibits.
1:00-2:00 p.m. Have lunch at the VMFA's Amuse Restaurant for convenient fine-dining after your tour.
2:30-4:30 p.m. Head to The Visual Arts Center of Richmond and enjoy free exhibits of the emerging and established artists of today.
4:30-6:30 p.m. Drive, bike, or walk around and check out the murals around the city on various buildings.The Richmond Mural Project has been bringing in artists from around the world to paint murals around the city in order to make Richmond a top art destination.
7:00-8:30 p.m. Finish your day with dinner at Balliceaux. There is dinner and live music every night except Sundays. has been bringing in artists from around the world to paint murals around the city in order to make Richmond a top art destination.
