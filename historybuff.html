The History Buff

Loving history and visiting Richmond is both good and bad. Good because there are so many historical places to explore, and bad because you will not be able to explore them all! Below are some of the attractions that will allow you to see the most on your visit.

8:30-10:00 a.m. Breakfast of your choice! Breakfast suggestions
10:00-12:00 p.m. Enjoy a Segway Tour that covers many historic locations downtown related to the Civil War, Richmond and Virginia Government, and Edgar Allan Poe.
12:30-1:00 p.m. Lunch at the historic and iconic Jefferson Hotel which opened in 1895 and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
1:00-3:00 p.m. Visit the Valentine, a museum that focuses on Richmond history.
3:00-5:00 p.m. Head to the Virginia Historical Society for a look into the history of the Commonwealth of Virginia.
5:00-7:00 p.m. Take a stroll through Hollywood Cemetery along the James River to see the burial sites of Presidents James Monroe and John Tyler, as well as some of the Confederacy's leaders and Generals (namely Jefferson Davis, J.E.B. Stuart, and George Pickett).
